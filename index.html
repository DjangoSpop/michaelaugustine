Overview

What is IAS 7?

IAS 7 ‚Äî Statement of Cash Flows ‚Äî is the International Accounting Standard that prescribes how entities must report information about historical changes in cash and cash equivalents. It requires companies to present a cash flow statement as a mandatory component of their complete set of financial statements. The standard ensures that financial statement users can evaluate the ability of an entity to generate cash and the way it uses those cash flows.

Under IAS 7, cash encompasses cash on hand and demand deposits, while cash equivalents are short-term, highly liquid investments readily convertible to known amounts of cash, with insignificant risk of change in value ‚Äî typically with a maturity of three months or less.

Objective

Core Purpose

The primary objective of IAS 7 is to require the presentation of information about an entity's historical changes in cash and cash equivalents by means of a cash flow statement. This classifies cash flows during the period according to operating, investing, and financing activities, enabling stakeholders to assess the entity's liquidity, solvency, and financial flexibility.

Scope

Who Does It Apply To?

IAS 7 applies to all entities that prepare financial statements in conformity with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). It is applicable regardless of an entity's nature, industry, or size ‚Äî covering banks, insurance companies, manufacturing firms, governments, and non-profit organisations alike.

The Three Core Activities

Operating Activities

Cash flows from the principal revenue-producing activities of the entity. These represent transactions that enter into the determination of profit or loss and include cash received from customers, cash paid to suppliers and employees, income taxes paid, and interest paid. Operating cash flows can be reported using the direct method (recommended) or the indirect method (widely used in practice).

Cash receipts from sales Payments to suppliers Employee wages paid Tax payments
üìà

Investing Activities

Cash flows related to the acquisition and disposal of long-term assets and other investments not included in cash equivalents. These reflect expenditures made for resources intended to generate future income and cash flows, helping users understand how the entity deploys capital to generate earnings over the long term.

Purchase of property & equipment Proceeds from asset sales Acquisition of subsidiaries Loans made to other parties
üè¶

Financing Activities

Cash flows that result in changes in the size and composition of the contributed equity and borrowings of the entity. These flows enable users to predict claims on future cash flows by providers of capital ‚Äî both debt and equity holders ‚Äî and assess the entity's capital structure strategy.

Proceeds from share issuance Dividends paid Borrowings raised Repayment of loans
Key Requirements

Presentation & Disclosures

Entities must disclose components of cash and cash equivalents and reconcile amounts in the cash flow statement with equivalent items in the balance sheet. IAS 7 also requires disclosure of significant non-cash transactions ‚Äî such as acquisition of assets by assuming liabilities ‚Äî and allows for certain cash flows like interest received and dividends to be classified under different activity headings, with consistency applied period to period.

Methods

Direct vs Indirect Method

Under the direct method, major classes of gross cash receipts and gross cash payments are disclosed ‚Äî providing more useful information but requiring additional data collection. Under the indirect method, net profit is adjusted for non-cash items (depreciation, amortisation) and changes in working capital. While IAS 7 encourages the direct method, the indirect method is predominant globally due to its practical simplicity.

Expert Insight

Why IAS 7 Matters in Business

From our consultancy experience, cash flow analysis under IAS 7 is often the most revealing diagnostic tool in financial due diligence. A company can report strong profits while haemorrhaging cash ‚Äî known as the "profit illusion." The IAS 7 statement strips away accounting estimates, accruals, and non-cash entries to show the raw financial heartbeat of a business.

At Michael Augustine & Associates, we apply IAS 7 frameworks in our investment advisory and corporate governance engagements to assess true liquidity, stress-test business models, and advise boards on sustainable cash management strategies across West African markets.
